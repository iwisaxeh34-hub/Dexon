Photography 101: Master Your Camera Settings

📅 June 11, 2026 ⏱ 7 min read

Learn aperture, shutter speed, ISO, and composition with simple exercises you can do today.

Read Full Article ↓

Understanding the exposure triangle is the foundation of great photography. Let's break it down.

The Exposure Triangle:

  • Aperture (f-stop): Controls depth of field. Lower f-number = blurrier background (good for portraits). Higher f-number = everything in focus (good for landscapes).
  • Shutter Speed: Controls motion blur. Fast = freeze action. Slow = motion blur (good for light trails).
  • ISO: Sensitivity to light. Low ISO = cleaner image. High ISO = brighter but more noise.

Exercise:

Set your camera to Manual mode. Take 3 photos of the same subject with different aperture settings. Compare the results. Do the same with shutter speed and ISO.

Practice daily and you'll master exposure in weeks!

Game Development for Beginners with Godot Engine

📅 June 8, 2026 ⏱ 9 min read

Create your first 2D game using the free and open-source Godot game engine.

Read Full Article ↓

Godot is one of the best free game engines available. It's lightweight, powerful, and uses its own scripting language called GDScript.

Getting Started with Godot:

  • Download Godot from the official website (free).
  • Create a new 2D project.
  • Add a Player (KinematicBody2D with a Sprite).
  • Write a simple movement script in GDScript.
  • Add enemies, collectibles, and a score system.

Sample GDScript Code:

extends KinematicBody2D
var speed = 200
func _process(delta):
    var velocity = Vector2()
    if Input.is_action_pressed("ui_right"): velocity.x += 1
    if Input.is_action_pressed("ui_left"): velocity.x -= 1
    if Input.is_action_pressed("ui_down"): velocity.y += 1
    if Input.is_action_pressed("ui_up"): velocity.y -= 1
    velocity = velocity.normalized() * speed
    move_and_collide(velocity * delta)

Godot's documentation is excellent. Follow their "Your First 2D Game" tutorial for a complete beginner guide.

Color Theory for Designers: A Practical Guide

📅 June 7, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read

Understand color harmonies, palettes, and psychology to improve your design work instantly.

Read Full Article ↓

Color is one of the most powerful tools in design. Understanding color theory can elevate your work from amateur to professional.

Color Harmonies:

  • Complementary: Colors opposite on the color wheel (red/green, blue/orange). Creates high contrast.
  • Analogous: Colors next to each other (blue, blue-green, green). Creates harmony and calm.
  • Triadic: Three colors equally spaced (red/yellow/blue). Vibrant and balanced.
  • Monochromatic: Variations of one color. Clean and professional.

Color Psychology:

  • Red: Energy, passion, urgency.
  • Blue: Trust, calm, professionalism.
  • Yellow: Optimism, happiness, attention.
  • Green: Nature, growth, health.
  • Purple: Luxury, creativity, mystery.

Use tools like Coolors or Adobe Color to create palettes. Test your designs on multiple devices to ensure color accuracy.

UI/UX Design Principles Every Developer Should Know

📅 June 5, 2026 ⏱ 7 min read

Boost your web projects with these essential user experience design principles.

Read Full Article ↓

Great design is invisible. Here are the UI/UX principles that will make your websites and apps delightful to use.

Key Design Principles:

  • Simplicity: Remove unnecessary elements. Less is more.
  • Consistency: Use the same colors, fonts, and patterns throughout.
  • Feedback: Let users know what's happening (loading states, hover effects).
  • Affordance: Make buttons look clickable, links look linkable.
  • Accessibility: Ensure your site works for everyone (contrast, screen readers).
  • Hierarchy: Guide the user's eye to the most important information.

Free Design Tools:

  • Figma (free tier) — Design and prototype.
  • Penpot — Open-source alternative to Figma.
  • Coolors — Generate color palettes.
  • FontPair — Find fonts that work together.

Remember: Design is how it works, not just how it looks.

Fashion photography combines art, technique, and storytelling. Here's how to excel.

  • Lighting: Natural light works best. Golden hour is your friend. Use reflectors to bounce light.
